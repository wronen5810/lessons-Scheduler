Chapter · 01

Welcome to saderOT

The app that organizes your teaching business – scheduling, students, payments, and communication, all in one place.

saderOT (Order for Life) is a purpose-built app for private tutors and music/language teachers. It lets you manage your schedule, receive booking requests from students, track payments, and stay in touch with everyone – all without manual spreadsheets or back-and-forth messages.

What can you do?

  • Schedule management – Set up recurring or one-time lesson slots and let students book themselves
  • Student management – A full student roster with details, grade, hourly rate, notes, and contacts
  • Payment tracking – See who paid, who owes, and how much – with one-click reminder messages
  • Direct messaging – Communicate with students via email, WhatsApp, or push notifications
  • Calendar events – Add exams, holidays, tasks, and personal appointments to your schedule
  • Groups – Manage group lessons with multiple students per slot
  • Student notebook – Track tasks, notes, resources, and grades per student
💡
Getting started in under 2 minutes

Just two actions to get running: (1) add at least one time slot to your schedule, and (2) add one student. That's your whole system up and running.

Chapter · 03

Schedule Management

The heart of the system – create time slots, manage calendar events, add lesson notes, and track every slot's status.

Calendar views

Switch between two views at the top of the calendar:

  • Weekly view – See the full week at a glance. Great for an overview
  • Day view – See a single day in detail. Useful for managing a busy day

To navigate between weeks, use the ← → arrows at the top, or click Today to jump back to the current week.

Adding a time slot

Click the + button on the calendar to open the slot creation form:

  1. Choose the slot type: Weekly or One-time Weekly – Repeats automatically every week on the same day and time. Perfect for regular students.
    One-time – Appears only on the specific date you choose. Use for special or replacement lessons.
  2. Set the time, duration, and day Choose a start time and lesson duration (your default from Settings is pre-filled). For weekly slots, choose the day of the week. For one-time slots, choose a specific date.
  3. Add a title (optional) For example "Piano lesson" or "Exam prep". Leave blank to show the slot without a title.
  4. Set the maximum number of students For private lessons, set to 1. For group lessons, choose a higher number to match your group size.
  5. End date for weekly slots (optional) If you want the recurring slot to stop on a specific date, set an end date.
  6. Click "Add slot" The slot appears on the calendar. A weekly slot generates occurrences for all future weeks.

Slot status colors

Every slot on the calendar is colored according to its current state:

Available (blue)
Open for student booking requests
Blocked (gray)
You blocked this slot – hidden from students
Pending (yellow)
A student's request waiting for your approval
Confirmed (purple)
Lesson is scheduled – hasn't happened yet
Completed (dark purple)
Lesson took place – waiting for payment mark
Paid (green)
Lesson has been paid for
Cancel request (red)
Student requested cancellation – needs your attention

Assigning a student to a slot

Click any available (blue) slot on the calendar. In the panel that opens:

  1. Click "Book for student" A list of your students opens.
  2. Select a student For multi-seat slots, you can assign multiple students to the same slot.
  3. Confirm the booking The slot immediately moves to "Confirmed" (purple) status.

Adding lesson notes

Any confirmed lesson lets you add notes. Click the lesson and scroll to the notes section at the bottom of the panel:

  • Write a note – Document what was covered, performance, or topics for the next lesson
  • Visible to student / Hidden – Choose whether the note appears in the student's portal
  • Delete a note – Click the delete button next to any note

Managing calendar events

In addition to lesson slots, you can add calendar events such as exams, holidays, meetings, and tasks. These appear on the calendar and help you plan ahead.

  1. Click an empty date on the calendar and choose "Add event" The event creation form opens.
  2. Choose the event type Exam, task, holiday, personal appointment, or any free-form description.
  3. Set date and time For multi-day events, set a separate end date.
  4. Assign to a student (optional) Link an event to a specific student – for example, a student's upcoming exam.
  5. Save The event appears on the calendar at the correct date.
📅
Sync with external calendars

You can sync your lesson schedule with Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook using an iCal link. Set this up in Settings → General tab → Calendar Sync. See Chapter 12 for details.

Chapter · 04

Student Management

All information about every student in one place – details, rate, contacts, notes, notebook, and more.

Go to Menu → Students to open your student list. The page has two tabs: Students and Groups.

Adding a new student

  1. Full name (required) The name shown in the schedule, notifications, and reports.
  2. Email address (optional) Used for sending messages and for student portal login.
  3. Phone number (optional) Used for WhatsApp and SMS messages.
  4. Grade (optional) Choose from a dropdown (grades 1–12). Displayed as a colored badge in the list.
  5. Click "Add student" The student is added to the list and is immediately active.

Filtering the list

Use the filter pills above the list:

  • All – Every student
  • Active – Currently active students only
  • Waiting – Students on the waitlist

Per-student actions (three-dot menu)

Click the three-dot menu next to any student to access all available actions:

ActionDescription
Edit student Edit name, email, phone, grade, hourly rate (₪), and private notes (not visible to the student)
Contacts Add and manage contacts – parents, guardians, emergency contacts. Set name, relationship, email, phone, and mark a primary contact
Notes View all lesson notes added for this student. Each note shows: date, time, and whether it's visible to the student (green) or hidden (gray)
Notebook Open the student's notebook (if the Notebook feature is enabled in Settings). Includes: tasks, notes, resources, and grades
Login history View the student's most recent portal login timestamps
Record payment Open a payment modal – enter an amount and optional note (e.g., "Cash", "Bank transfer")
Remove student Removes the student from your list after confirmation. Does not affect existing lessons

Changing student status

Click the status badge next to any student to change it:

  • Active (green) – A regular student currently learning
  • Waiting (amber) – On the waitlist
  • Inactive (gray) – No longer learning, kept in archive

Hourly rate

Each student's profile supports an individual hourly rate (₪). This rate is used in billing reports. If no rate is set, the student appears as "No rate" in the billing page.

📥
Bulk Import

Have a long student list? Use bulk import: Menu → Students → Import. You can import from a spreadsheet (Excel / CSV) with names, emails, and phone numbers.

🔒
When to use Contacts

If you teach young students, add the parent's details as a contact. This lets you message parents directly and reach them quickly when needed.

Chapter · 05

Sharing Your Link

Send one link – students see your schedule and request lessons themselves.

Every teacher has a unique personal booking link. When a student visits this link, they see your available time slots and can submit a lesson request. You just approve it.

Finding your link

  1. Click "Share" in the menu Your personal link is displayed with a "Copy" button.
  2. Click "Copy" The link is copied to your clipboard.
  3. Send it to your students Via WhatsApp, SMS, email – whatever works for you. You only need to send it once.

New students – join request flow

A student who isn't in your list yet will need to submit a join request first:

  1. Student clicks your link They see a page with your name, subject area, and photo (if set in your profile).
  2. They submit a join request They fill in their name and details. You receive a notification.
  3. You approve the request From the dashboard or notification. Once approved, the student is automatically added to your list and can start booking lessons.
⚡
Auto-approve students

Enable Auto-approve in Settings so new students are added instantly without manual approval. See Chapter 12.

🔗
One link for everyone

You don't need separate links per student – one link works for all. Existing students (already in your list) see the schedule immediately. New students go through the join flow.

Chapter · 06

Lesson Requests & Approval

Students request – you approve. Simple, fast, always in your control.

When a student visits your link and chooses a time slot, the request arrives automatically and appears on your calendar in yellow (Pending).

How to approve or decline a request

  1. Click the yellow slot on the calendar A panel opens with the request details: student name, date, time, and lesson type.
  2. Click "Approve" or "Decline" Approve: Status changes to purple (Confirmed), the student receives a notification.
    Decline: The slot returns to blue (Available), the student is notified.
  3. After the lesson – mark as Completed Click "Mark as completed" in the slot panel. The color changes to dark purple. This enables payment tracking.

Cancellation requests from students

If you've enabled the "Cancellation Requests" feature in Settings (see Chapter 12), students can request to cancel a lesson. The request appears in red on the calendar:

  • Approve the cancellation – The lesson is cancelled, the slot reopens as available
  • Deny the cancellation – The lesson stays confirmed, the student is notified
🔔
Never miss a request

Enable push notifications (see Chapter 11) to receive an instant alert for every new lesson request.

Chapter · 07

Booking & Blocking Slots

Manually book a student, block personal time, and edit or delete existing slots.

Click any slot on the calendar to open the action panel. What's available depends on the slot's current status.

Actions on an available slot

  1. Book for student Choose a student from your list and book them directly – no request needed. Useful when a student asks by phone or WhatsApp.
  2. Block this slot Turns the slot gray (Blocked) and hides it from students. Use for private meetings, errands, holidays, or any time you're unavailable.

Editing and deleting

  1. Edit slot Change the start time, duration, title, max students, or (for weekly slots) the end date.
  2. Delete slot For weekly slots: choose "Delete this occurrence only" or "Delete all future occurrences from today".
    For one-time slots: immediately deleted after confirmation.
  3. Cancel a confirmed lesson Click "Cancel lesson". For recurring (weekly) lessons, you can set a last-lesson date – all lessons from that date onward will be cancelled.
⚠️
Blocking vs. deleting

Blocking keeps the slot on the calendar but hides it from students. You can unblock it at any time. Deleting removes it permanently. When in doubt, block – you can always unblock later.

Chapter · 08

Groups

Manage group lessons – multiple students, one slot.

Groups let you assign several students to the same time slot and track their payments individually. Go to the Groups tab on the Students page.

Creating a new group

  1. Click "Add group" Give the group a unique name, e.g. "Grade 3 Math" or "Guitar – Thursday group".
  2. Set a group rate (optional) Enter a total lesson rate for the whole group. The system automatically calculates each student's share.
  3. Save the group The group is created empty. Add members next.

Managing group members

Click "Members" next to the group:

  • Add a member – Choose any student from your general student list
  • Remove a member – Click "Remove" next to the student's name. Does not affect already-recorded lessons
  • View members – List with name and email for each member

Group notebook

If the Notebook feature is enabled, each group can have its own notebook with shared tasks, notes, and content for all group members.

Editing and deleting a group

Click the three-dot menu next to the group:

  • Edit – Change the group name and rate
  • Delete – Removes the group. Past lessons are not deleted
👥
Set the right max students

When creating a weekly slot for a group lesson, set Max students to match your group size. This prevents overbooking.

Chapter · 09

Billing & Payments

Full payment tracking – who paid, who owes, send reminders, and generate receipts.

Go to Menu → Billing. The billing page is split into two sections: individual students and groups.

Summary cards

  • Total unpaid (₪) – Combined amount owed across all completed, unpaid lessons
  • Lesson count – Total completed lessons, broken down by students and groups

Individual student billing

For each student you'll see: lesson count, hourly rate, outstanding balance, and any unallocated credits (if they've overpaid).

Click a student's name to expand and see a full table of all their lessons with date, time, and status.

Recording a payment

  1. Click the + icon next to the student's name The Record Payment modal opens.
  2. Enter the amount (₪) Required field.
  3. Add a note (optional) For example: "Cash", "Bank transfer", "Bit", "PayPal".
  4. Click "Save" The payment is recorded and the student's balance updates immediately.

Sending a payment reminder

For students with an outstanding balance, send a reminder in one click:

  1. Click the bell icon next to the student's name A reminder modal opens with a pre-written message including the student's name and balance.
  2. Choose delivery channel(s) Check one or more: Email, WhatsApp, Notification.
  3. Edit the message (optional) The message is fully editable before sending.
  4. Click "Send" The message is sent. The last-reminder date is saved and displayed in the list.

Generating a receipt

  1. Click the receipt icon next to the student's name A modal opens showing all payments for that student.
  2. Select a payment and click "Generate receipt" A printable receipt document opens.
  3. Print or save as PDF Use your browser's print function to save as a PDF file.

Group billing

In the Groups billing section, each group shows completed lessons, the per-student per-lesson rate, and the balance for each member. You can send a reminder to each member individually using the bell icon next to their name.

Receipt search

At the top of the billing page, use the search tool: select a student from the dropdown and click "Open" to see their full payment history and all receipts.

💰
Export to Excel

You can export all billing data to a complete Excel file from Settings → Export tab. The file includes lessons, payments, students, and notebooks. See Chapter 12.

Chapter · 10

Messages

Direct communication with students – an inbox and a multi-channel compose tool.

Go to Menu → Messages. The page has two tabs: Inbox and Compose.

Inbox tab

Shows all conversations with students, grouped by student. For each conversation:

  • Student name and email
  • Preview of the latest message
  • Date of the last message
  • Blue badge with unread count (if any)

Click a conversation to open it. The full message thread is shown chronologically, with sender and timestamp for each message.

Compose tab

To send a message to one or more students at once:

  1. Select recipients Search students by name in the left panel, check them. Use "Select all" to message every student, or select an entire Group.
  2. Write your message Type in the textarea on the right panel.
  3. Choose delivery channel(s) Toggle one or more:
    • Email ✉️ – Sent to the student's email address
    • WhatsApp 💬 – Opens a send link per recipient (requires phone number)
    • Notification 🔔 – Sent as a push notification (requires student to have notifications enabled)
  4. Click "Send" The message is sent. A results list shows how many succeeded, and lists any failures with reasons.

Message templates

Save time on repeated messages (reminders, confirmations, routine notices) with templates. Go to Menu → Templates:

  • Add template – Write a message and save it with a name
  • Use a template – When composing, select a template from the list to load it instantly
  • Edit / Delete templates – Manage your template library
📱
Sending via WhatsApp

WhatsApp sends open a link for each recipient individually. Make sure every student you're messaging via WhatsApp has a phone number saved. Students without a number will appear in a "Not sent" list.

Chapter · 11

Push Notifications

Get an instant alert for every important event – even when the app is closed.

What triggers a notification?

  • A new student submitted a join request
  • An existing student requested a lesson
  • A student requested lesson cancellation
  • A new message arrived from a student
  • An upcoming lesson reminder (based on your settings)

How to enable notifications

  1. Open the app on your device On Android – from the installed app. On iPhone – from Safari (add to Home Screen first).
  2. Click "Allow" when the permission prompt appears The browser or app will request notification permission. Tap "Allow".
  3. Verify notifications are enabled In your device settings, confirm that saderOT is listed as an app allowed to send notifications.

Notification preferences

In Settings → General → Notification Preferences, choose exactly which events should be sent to which channel:

  • Lesson request – Email / WhatsApp / Notification
  • Lesson approved – Email / WhatsApp / Notification
  • Lesson declined – Email / WhatsApp / Notification
  • Lesson cancelled – Email / WhatsApp / Notification
  • Lesson reminder – Email / WhatsApp / Notification
  • Access request – Email / WhatsApp / Notification
📱
iPhone users

On iPhone, open saderOT in Safari, tap the Share button, and choose "Add to Home Screen". Then open the app from your home screen and allow notifications when prompted. Push notifications on iOS require Safari 16.4+ (iOS 16.4 or later).

🔔
Not receiving notifications?

Check: (1) Notifications are enabled for saderOT in your device settings, (2) your device is not in silent/do-not-disturb mode, (3) you've enabled the desired notification types in saderOT Settings.

FAQ

Answers to the most common questions.

How does a student sign up?

Two ways: (1) You add them manually in the Students page (Menu → Students), or (2) you send them your personal link – they fill in their details and submit a join request. Once you approve, they're automatically added to your list.

What's the difference between a weekly and a one-time slot?

Weekly – Automatically created every week on the same day and time. Perfect for recurring students. One-time – Only appears on the specific date you chose. Use for special lessons, make-up sessions, or irregular scheduling.

Can students see my entire calendar?

No. Students only see slots with Available status (blue). Blocked slots, other students' lessons, and personal events are all hidden from the student view.

How do I cancel a confirmed lesson?

Click the lesson on the calendar, and in the panel click "Cancel lesson". For recurring (weekly) lessons, you can set a last-lesson date – all lessons from that date onward are cancelled. You can notify the student manually via the Messages page.

How does payment tracking work?

Once you mark a lesson as "Completed", it appears in the Billing page as an open debt. When you record a payment for that student, it's matched to their completed lessons and the balance updates automatically.

How do I set a different rate for a specific student?

In the Students page: click the three-dot menu next to the student → Edit student → "Hourly rate" field. This overrides the default rate in Settings for that student only.

How do I sync the schedule with Google Calendar?

Settings → General → Calendar Sync → Generate link. Copy the link. In Google Calendar: Settings → Add calendar → From URL. Paste the link and confirm. The calendar syncs automatically.

Can I manage group lessons?

Yes. First enable the Groups feature in Settings → General → Features. Then create a group in Students → Groups tab, add members, and create a time slot with max students set to match your group size.

Can I export my data?

Yes. Go to Settings → Export tab. Download an Excel file with all your data: students, lessons, payments, and notebooks. The file opens in both Excel and Google Sheets.

Is there a mobile app?

Android: The app is available for direct installation. iPhone: Open saderOT in Safari, tap "Share" → "Add to Home Screen". This gives you a full app experience including push notifications (requires iOS 16.4+).

How do I contact support?

Reach us at support@saderot.com. We aim to respond within 24 hours.
